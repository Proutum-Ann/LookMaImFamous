Edgar Allan Poe

Edgar Allan Poe

Edgar Allan Poe was known as an American writer, editor, literary critic, and most famously of all, a poet. He's often well regarded in modern times, despite the hiccups he's had in life, and is remembered today as a revolutionary poet.

Early Years

Edgar Allan Poe, son of famous actors, and orphaned at three. He would go on to being raised by a prosperous Virginian exporter by the name of John Allan. He'd lived a rather educated childhood, but that would end once he started college in the University of Virginia, where he had to drop out due to insufficient funding from Allan and generally not being financially stable.

Edgar Allan Poe would later move to Boston and pursue a military career, in which he would be honorably discharged as a regimental sergeant major after publishing his first poetry collection, Tamerlane, and Other Poems.. He was later sent to complete his education and admitted to the United States Military Academy at West Point. He would run into a financial roadblock again, however, as Allan refused to pay for the school and did not give consent for Allan Poe to go to the academy. He was later dismissed due to ignoring his duties and violating regulations at the academy

He would leave for New York city, publishing a few more poems before settling down for a few years with his aunt, Mrs. Maria Clemm, in Baltimore. After quite a bit of moving, Allan Poe would finally settle in the state of New York.
